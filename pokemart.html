Poké Mart

Davina’s Poké Mart Delivery is a location-based mobile app concept that allows Pokémon items to be delivered wherever you are.

The Challenge

When Pokémon trainers run out of items, they must travel far and wide to restock at a Poké Mart in town. This is a concern for trainers who have run out of items and must journey through a dangerous area to reach the nearest Poké Mart.

Therefore, the UX must be simple and timesaving to assure trainers and their Pokémon can acquire items quickly and in any location.

My Role:

  • Illustration/Icon Designs
  • User Research & Analysis
  • Persona Creation
  • User Stories & MVP
  • Wireframes
  • UI Design & Prototyping

RESEARCH &
PROBLEM DEFINITION

User Survey

To begin my research, I conducted a short, 10-question user survey to gain insight on users that matched the target user profile. I received 13 responses from past Pokémon players.

Results

  • 31% disliked having to constantly go to Pokè Marts to restock on items all the time.
    61% found it annoying but were okay with it.
    Solution: Create a usable and memorable app that users will not get bored of.

  • 54% would only wait under 10 minutes for a delivery and 23% would wait 10-20 minutes for a delivery.
    Solution: Use location to find the nearest Pokè Marts to have faster delivery.

  • 50% purchase Pokèballs the most at Pokè Marts, followed by 20% purchasing health potions the most at Pokè Marts.
    Solution: Add a ‘most frequently purchased’ section at the top of the screen.

Personas

Based on the user research I set up two personas. I referred to them throughout the entire product development process.



  • Each persona had a scenario that identified a realistic end goal, as well as the user’s motivations and frustrations.

  • The information about each persona focused on the user’s interaction. with the product, ultimately affecting my design decisions throughout the process.

User Stories & MVP

After identifying the users’ main goals and behaviours through user research and persona creation, I found that the main issues my design decisions needed to solve involved:


  • The speed of delivery based on location
  • Delivery time availabilities
  • Ability to purchase any item from a Pokè Mart

I was able to create user stories and define the MVP based on this information.

IDEATING SOLUTIONS

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

I began the design process by sketching rough ideas on paper to quickly find alternative setups that worked best.

Medium-Fidelity Wireframes

I turned my revised sketches into an interactive prototype done with Adobe XD. I defined the UI elements, visual hierarchy and design patterns.

High-Fidelity Mockups

After more research and usability testing, I designed the final screens with Adobe XD. The process involved a lot of trial and error with the design choices, as this was my first mobile app design. My aim was to create a clean, modern aesthetic to help users fulfil their goals easier and quicker. The onboarding illustrations were designed in Affinity Designer to stick with the playful theme of colours and Pokémon feel.

DESIGN

UI Elements

Logo

I designed a few logos around the concept of a Poké ball and motion. The Speed Lines version created motion however, it was unclear that it was for a delivery app. The Delivery Outfit version was too complex for a logo and would not work well in smaller sizes.

I decided on the Arrow version because it represents deliveries around the world, where the Poké ball is portrayed as a globe.

Colours & Typography

To respect the theme of the product, I have made careful decisions in terms of colours and typography.

Vector Elements

For the products of this app, I used Affinity Designer to create vector illustrations of the major 25 items found in Poké Marts.
